Google Ads Mobilization Plan

A Strategic Guide for MyBestPicks.ca

Phase 1: Foundational Setup (Pre-Launch)

This is the most critical phase. Proper setup ensures we can accurately track performance, optimize for sales, and legally run product ads. Do not skip any of these steps.

1 Account Integration

  • Create & verify Google Merchant Center.
  • Link Merchant Center to Google Ads.
  • Link Google Analytics 4 (GA4) to Google Ads.
  • Ensure billing information is correct in Google Ads.

2 Product Feed Optimization

Your product feed is your lifeline for Shopping ads. Upload it to Merchant Center with:

  • High-quality, clean images.
  • SEO-rich titles and descriptions.
  • Accurate pricing (in CAD) and availability.
  • GTINs/MPNs where possible to improve matching.

3 Conversion Tracking

Set up conversion tracking via GA4 or the Google Ads tag. Without this, we are flying blind. Track:

  • Purchase: Primary goal, track transaction value.
  • Add to Cart: Key micro-conversion.
  • Begin Checkout: High-intent signal.

Phase 2: Campaign Structure & Strategy

We will use a modern, multi-pronged approach focused on Google's AI to find converting customers efficiently. This structure maximizes reach while maintaining control.

Primary Campaign: Performance Max (PMax)

This will be our main engine for sales. PMax uses AI to run ads across all of Google's networks (Shopping, Search, YouTube, etc.). It's ideal for e-commerce.

  • Goal: Sales.
  • Asset Groups: Create groups based on your product categories (e.g., 'Home Gadgets', 'Outdoor Gear'). Provide high-quality images, logos, and ad copy.
  • Audience Signals: This is crucial. Guide the AI by providing signals like website visitors (remarketing), and in-market audiences for your product types.

Supporting Campaign: Dynamic Search Ads (DSA)

DSA helps capture long-tail keyword searches you might miss. Google dynamically creates ads based on your website's content.

  • Goal: Capture broad, relevant search traffic.
  • Targeting: Target your product category pages.
  • Negative Keywords: Actively add negative keywords (e.g., 'free', 'jobs', 'reviews') to avoid wasted spend.

Initial Campaign Focus

Our initial budget and effort will be heavily weighted towards Performance Max, as it's the most effective tool for driving sales in e-commerce today.

Phase 3: Bidding & Budget Strategy

Our approach is to start conservatively, gather data, and then scale intelligently based on performance. This minimizes initial risk and builds a foundation for profitable growth.

Initial Budget

Start with a modest daily budget of $20 - $30 CAD. This is enough to gather initial data without significant risk. We can scale this up once we see positive returns.

Bidding Strategy: A Two-Step Process

  1. Learning Phase (First 2-4 weeks): Use the "Maximize Conversions" bid strategy. The goal here is purely data collection. We want Google's AI to learn who your customers are by getting as many sales as possible, even if the initial return isn't high.
  2. Profitability Phase (After 20+ conversions): Switch to "Target ROAS" (Return On Ad Spend). Now that we have data, we can tell Google our profitability target (e.g., "For every $1 I spend, I want $3 back"). The AI will then optimize bids to hit this goal.

Targeting

Target all of Canada. Initially, we won't segment further. Once we have data, we can analyze performance by province and apply bid adjustments if certain areas perform significantly better.

Bidding & Budget Scaling

The strategy evolves from data gathering to profit-driven scaling over the first 60 days.

Phase 4: Ongoing Optimization & Scaling

Google Ads is not "set it and forget it." Consistent optimization is key to long-term profitability. Here is your weekly and monthly checklist.

Weekly Checklist

  • ✔
    Review Search Terms (for DSA): Check the search query report. Add any irrelevant terms (e.g., "how to fix", "jobs") as negative keywords to stop wasting money.
  • ✔
    Check PMax Asset Performance: In your PMax asset group, see which images and headlines are performing best. Pause the "Low" performing assets and test new variations.
  • ✔
    Monitor Budget Pacing: Ensure your daily budget is being spent fully. If not, your bids or targeting may be too restrictive.

Monthly Checklist & Scaling

  • ✔
    Analyze Product Performance: In Merchant Center or the Ads UI, identify your best-selling products. Consider creating a separate "High Performers" campaign for them to allocate more budget and control.
  • ✔
    Review Audience Insights: See which audience segments are converting best. Use this data to refine your audience signals in PMax.
  • ✔
    Scaling Strategy: If your campaigns are profitable and hitting your Target ROAS goal, begin scaling. Increase the daily budget by 15-20% every 3-4 days. Avoid large, sudden increases as this can reset the algorithm's learning phase.

This interactive plan provides a strategic framework. Success requires consistent monitoring and data-driven adjustments.
